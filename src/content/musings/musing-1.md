I want to write to people. But I don’t write to label myself as a Writer. I am not a writer. I have no clue how a writer’s mind works. Writing for me isn’t a profession, a passion or even a hobby.

I write because I have no other way to communicate my feelings, my thoughts, and my emotions. I don’t converse my happiness, sorrow or anger. That doesn’t mean I am insensitive. Neither am I devoid of sentiment.

I am a terribly sensitive, fragile, affectionate, egotistical and easily offended person who finds happiness in little things and craves for external validation. Every time a person gets to know the real me, I’ve watched them be surprised.

I often write out loud. But I don’t want you to tell me it’s a good piece of writing, or bad. I don’t want any feedback on my writings at all. I want you to understand me through them. It’s a call for attention for sure. I want to write to every person scrolling through their feed. I want to feel that the world is there to listen to me.

Then I get worried about the judgements I might possibly receive. When I ramble about the way I think, feel, or process my emotions, I don’t want you to think I am a weird person. I don’t want you to read my words as exaggerated metaphors for prose poetry.

I want you to understand the difficulty I face, for the things that are supposed to be easy.

I want to write to people.
