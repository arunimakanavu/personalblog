I had a dream last night.

I was back at my school.

Not unlike the old days, I have been constantly questioned about my performance, exams scores, the pretentious occurance of "luck" in every exams I scored well, the laziness to use my "real potential" for every exams I scored poorly.

My school life, for many reasons, was the one timeframe of my life I never want to revisit, even to try and fix things up.

Then there was this curious little girl who used to follow me around asking questions (she's still one of the never-to-be-forgetten faces in my life, even though I know she must have a completely different, grown-up look now).

As we were sitting on a bench near the play ground, she asked me, "I've been watching you all these years in your 20's. You were always alone. How do you get along with that?"

I get this question a lot in my real life, from very random people or even strangers. And every time I just smiled along.

This time I answered.

"Perhaps, solitude isn't as bad as people think. Solitude is the phase where you don't have to prove your existence, or anything about yourself at all, to others. You get away from the constant criticism, questions and judgements. You're no longer obligated to answer people.

Regardless, you focus on yourself. You work on yourself. You take time growing to what you actually want to become. Because, you'll realise, you're not a showpiece for anyone anymore.

You might question yourself, but you'll also get the answers from yourself. You'll have the best talks of your life and you'll see it's just you on the other side of that conversation.

Perhaps solitude is the time of life where you finally give peace to yourself."
