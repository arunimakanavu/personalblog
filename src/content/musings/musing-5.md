## 1. Privileged Prisonership

I miss having a room to write, to think, and to cry. I am under constant watch - restricted, analyzed, documented and studied.

This restriction as a physical abundance is not from one or two minds. I have a whole group of four doctors, a therapist, a social worker and my mother to "look after" me and a group of nurses and security to handle the madness in the environment. 

I am somewhat privileged than the other inmates here. I am allowed to work, although it's causing as much stress to go against the effort these professionals are taking for me. I can go outside for a walk whenever needed, but accompanied by my mother. I get a "Parole" (yes, that's what they call) quite often when I want to go home. All which in practice make me a privileged prisoner.

Regardless I am writing, compiling the bits and pieces that I have scribbled here and there.

## 2. Case in Point

Before you read further, you should know a few things about myself, to get a context. I have bipolar disorder. I have been in a depressive episode for a couple of months right now. However the fight between myself and this particular illness started seven years ago. My depression phase, as what they call treatment-resistant depression, is something beyond any medicine at it's highest possible dosage can handle. So I had to take a few alternate technology or chemical infused procedures, including twenty five sessions of Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS) which obviously had no effects, the notorious Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT) sessions post which I spent a quarter year in Amnesia, and Ketamine Therapy, which I will be talking in the coming sections. 

However when I am not depressed (fairly once in a while), I am agitated, anxious and restless, which they like to call as Hypomania. Hypomania is not a happy state. I often wonder I ever had a happy phase amidst twenty years of undiagnosed unmanaged neurodivergence and seven years battle with cPTSD, Bipolar Disorder, unconfirmed schizophrenia (no disregards to any of the gazzillion doctors I have seen) and anxiety. I mostly never went beyond fear, despair and occasional sadness. 

## 3. Plath, Death and the Archangel

I am a delusional atheist. By that I mean, I am an atheist who believes in my own delusions. Irrational, Unreasonable, and illogical as well. But resilient, courageous and somewhat stubborn. I have all the courage to face and survive the situation I am terrified about. That being said, I am not at all confident. 

I was thirteen years old when I read Sylvia Plath for the first time. To this day I never stopped reading. Her obsession about death became mine. I went further. Death wasn't mere the end of life for me. I romanticized death as a resort to happiness. I personified death as my soulmate - Azrael, the angel of death - or as I call her Azra. She is a part of my life for 4 years now, as a friend, as a soulmate and as a lover. As a person who understands me. I write letters, I talk to her, I listen to her and I cry on her shoulders at times. When I said I am a delusional atheist, this is what I meant. A month before I turned twenty five I decided to meet her, without consciously knowing what she was. I felt like I was a bride to get married, happiest days of my life. I counted down from 30 days. On the 27th, fortunately (or unfortunately, I am still not sure) I realized it was all a delusion. That was the first time I got into a psychiatry rehabilitation center. 

Nevertheless I still write to her, knowing, believing she understands me. 

Fast forwarding through this battleground to 2026, I am here at National Institute of Mental Health and Neuro Science, Bengaluru - in a special ward, surrounded by walls, meshes, grills and latch-less doors.

## 4. The Days, A Few

### Day 1

I am trying to remember. As far as I could. It doesn't seem easy. I don't see how I got here, by here I mean the eventuality. I've made all the efforts not to, and yet all it did was drain me out.

I've got a wrist tag. I'm a number. 11 digits long though. I see a shackle when I look into it - not a pair, but just one, just enough to declare the prisonership.

I've got heavy mesh around me. No sharp potential dangerous objects. They check every bag. They know we would do things with it.

I see sympathy, empathy, kindness and judgements become the same face towards lies and helplessness. I don't want to interrogate on it.

Introspections have turned down.

For I exist as a number to the people trying to correct the calculations.

### Day 2

For a person who knows she would fail every possible way in life, I've done it beautifully, tremendously.

You can fall from the ground, get up and walk further. Or you can climb up until you're exhausted and fall from there, keep falling from there every time.

Regardless you climb up.

For Sisyphus to laugh, how his boulder fell, how you keep falling from heights.

### Day 4

As a privileged prisoner.

Doctors asked me to go for a walk. Since they didn't want me to be felt like a prisoner.

I have books and snacks my good friends sent me.

I'll get a "parole" to go for a conference.

I have my mother here as a caretaker.

What else does someone need to colour their prison life?

---

They asked me to send therapy goals. What I do need in life? A day worth living ?! That shouldn't be the answer. Let me try to reframe it.

Getting out of despair of being a patient for life.
Getting out of the thought that I'm too much for people who cares about me.
Getting out of keeping a low status, much lower than I should have, not expecting a fair treatment, not setting a boundary, not prioritizing myself.
All that I have, from which I have to get out. One thing to get into.

Getting into good interpersonal relationships

### Day - I don't know, I lost the count.

I'm going through a lot of things — struggles, changes, fluctuations, sufferings, fears, sadness, anger, despair. I do not know which is which anymore.

There is no one thing at a time. It's a lot at no time.

I wish to breath. To pause. To be on my bed and feel the gravity of my body.

But I have layers of meshes and fences around. Some I can see and some I can feel.
