I often wonder what matters in an interpersonal connection - physical or emotional availability? Perhaps what should matter more? This is something that I should ask myself, not as a receiver,but as a giver too. 

It seems in a digital-interaction-dominant world emotional availability often defined as sparing a few moments of virtual presence as needed by the other person. Which is of course not something that needs a lot of efforts. However times are changing and these few moments of virtual availability is also something that we seek for. When I am saying this I know I am one of those people who appears to be the most unavailable in any given definitions of emotional, physical or virtual presence. 

I care about people a lot. I worry about them a lot. But the fear of getting hurt or getting overwhelmed, in me, has built a fortress to prevent any emotion from flowing out, or in perhaps. I am often hesitant to let people available for me.

I overthink. But unlike introspection vast majority of it would be the way people feel about me or perceive the actions or words or even silence that comes out of me. Rather I don’t believe anyone who’s callous, inconsiderate or even carefree can overthink. 

In my case the drill of overthinking follows a temporal structure that includes one or maximum two people mostly. It takes a situation, build up at least five different narratives where I get hurt, they get hurt, nothing happens, I get happy or they get happy.Every give and take based on the behavioral pattern I gathered up on a retrospective basis serve as a baseline data for this. I am not a nihilistic or pessimistic person, but I do consider the climax of me being happy as lesser known and them being happy as the best possible outcome. This can overlaps with a person I care being in my life - the correlation to causality linearity.  

But real life situations are always non-linear, chaotic like the butterfly effect, diverging and not involving one or two variables eventually for the ease of calculation. Although mostly most of them seems to be converging to a neutral point where no one gets affected positively or negatively. 

I find this an undesirable outcome - so I assume the other person gets hurt and tends to crucify myself for causing it. 

This aftermath is the real cause of my struggle to establish a connection - an expressive one in a real world that often gets validated as affection or commitments, or availability perhaps. 

I am closing this rant with a quote from Sylvia Plath:

“I am a victim of Introspection”
