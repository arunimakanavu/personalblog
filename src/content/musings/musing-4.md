Watching me goofing around and daydreaming, time asked, "Do you want me to change the speed ?"

Irking me has been a habit now. I was sitting in the very corner of this train compartment, staring at scenery, the broken prospects, through the window. I couldn't disregard the sun shining on my face although all I could see are some pieces of sky. The wind was rushing out through, trying to take my untied hair strands along with it.

I couldn't care less to that question. But after a moment I thought of answering.

"I don't want you to be around anymore."

I wasn't even cared look at her, but I knew it'd puzzle her. It was a very unusual answer for a usual question.

"You really don't want me anymore ? After all these years we were together."

"Together ?"

"Spent. Well spent."

"I was your slave for twenty-five years."

"I was helping you"

"You were poisoning me. The only thing I could rely was speed. The only hope. But eventually, you turned everything into chaos. To the point where I wanted to quite, but that didn't happen either." I was slowly getting indignant.

"Well, what are you gonna do without me ? Without me speed has no purpose."

"I'm okay to live without a purpose." The speed who was listening to us said in a low voice. "At least I won't have to bend, strain and distort bounded by you."

"I want you to cut this handcuffs you put on us forced me to believe it's for my good." I said in my stern voice I barely used before. Seemed a little harsh too. "I will never let you drag me along anymore."

Although she wasn't expecting it, it was clear from her face that, she was quite aware of it. That realisation made me even enraged.

"Well, I'll remove these cuffs. But can I just walk with you ?"

I didn't say anything. I knew she was manipulating me. But, yeah, she removed the cuffs and just sit there silently.

I pretended.

Now the journey is mine.
