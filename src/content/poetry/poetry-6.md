The souvenirs of Obliterated Miles

I looked at the long walkway behind.  
The sojourns I once used to unwound,  
The woods I once hang out carefree,  
The hilltop I watched the hues of horizon,  
The meadows where I gazed at the stars,  
The beach I larked around with waves,  
Built sand castles, embraced by the wind,

Have vanished, forever,  
Like it never existed.  
The wooden benches,  
Where I once used to wait,  
Where I've once been waited by,  
Had the most contagious laughs,  
Had the moments to live for,  
Seemed to have mouldered now.

I saw the pavement I came,  
Felt it was calling me back.  
I walked back, out of an instinct, perhaps.  
A few plants, not withered yet  
Seemed to beg me to get drenched.  
I felt reluctant and was no more  
Interested in prospering that garden.  
Yet I yearned for a rain.

I wandered around and picked up  
Some bits and pieces I wanted to keep.  
The souvenirs, broken, shrivelled.  
Put them in my bag, carefully.  
Maybe I could decorate,  
When I find my home, ever.
