I want to spend the rest of my time  
Lingering where I can watch  
A gazillion butterflies flying around,  
Embracing their life.  
Celebrating their days.  
In a garden full of their desired plants  
To host and to bloom for them.

I want them to fly around me,  
To kiss on my cheeks and neck.  
To perch on my hair strands  
And embellish my hair with their wings.  
I'll take them on my palms and  
Feel the tickling of those tiny limbs.

I will preserve their eggs,  
Look after and feed the caterpillars,  
Safeguard them throughout their pupae,  
I want to see them breaking it,  
Stretching those ravishing wings  
To spread out as the daylight strikes and  
Flutter away for another phase to begin.

Let me indulge in those flitting colors, and  
Forget the deep obscure fear inside, forever.  
Let me never agonize over the despair and  
The poor prospects I've been foreseeing.   
Let those tints and tones fill in the heart  
And flow in my veins throughout the body,  
Instead of the blinding pervaded darkness.  
I'll finally find my peace amidst the flora  
In my entire perplexing existence.

You can chain me there.  
I would never realise it.  
Even if you fasten the cuffs tight enough  
To kill my muscles and blood vessels,  
I would never feel the soreness.  
Even if I'm forbidden to have a sight  
Of the rest of the world outside,  
I would never try to escape.

Nevertheless some day,  
My body will start to disintegrate, leisurely.  
Each of the small fragments will mutate  
Into butterflies with bewitching wings,  
With the hues once flowed through me.  
They will spread those wings and  
Soar into the sky, as far as they can

Until the last butterfly wing away,  
I'll be in your shackles.  
Until the last bit of my metamorphosis.  
I'll live peacefully in your harness.  
You can chain me there.
