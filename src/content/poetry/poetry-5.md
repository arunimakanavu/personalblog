Speranza, the fallen kingdom.  
I usually wander around, barefooted.  
I won't ask you to be my guest here.  
But I can't latch these rusted gates either.  
The light rarely reaches here. Although,  
You won't see the stars, amongst the clouds.  
Neither the moon nor a shooting star  
Would light this place at night.

The meadow you get into past the gates,  
Used to be verdant and flourishing.  
So as the withered garden alongside.  
It hasn't drizzled in a long while,  
Despite this heavily overcast sky.  
The Paddock has been empty since the  
Cavalrymen and chargers starved to death.

This bridleway leads to the graveyard of my dreams  
Which is guarded by vicious nightmares.  
I don't want you to go there.

I'll let you walk through this esplanade  
But the harbor you will reach, where  
My fortune and serendipity used to anchor,  
Has been ravaged by the hurricane.  
It also wrecked my ships.

Followed by these stairs, there's a dungeon,  
Where I've clasped my fate in irons.  
The chateau of sentience,  
Where I dwelt and reigned the kingdom once,  
Now haunted by the bugbears of dread and angst.

And finally the desert of survivorship.  
Will you find an oasis for me to breathe ?
